About Lyndishes

A Culinary Institute Built for the Next Generation

Lyndishes Culinary School is a professional culinary institute based in Gwarimpa, Abuja, dedicated to training the next generation of chefs and food entrepreneurs.

Founded by Chef Lyn Ewalefoh, a City & Guilds certified chef trained at the International Centre for Culinary Arts (ICCA), Dubai, Lyndishes was created to bridge the gap between culinary skills and real food business knowledge.

At Lyndishes, students receive hands-on training in professional cooking techniques, kitchen management, food presentation, and culinary creativity. Beyond cooking, the school places strong emphasis on entrepreneurship, helping students understand how to build sustainable and profitable food businesses.

Since its establishment, Lyndishes Culinary School has trained thousands of students who are now working as chefs, bakers, caterers, and food business owners.

  • Hands-on professional culinary education
  • Practical pastry, baking, and kitchen management training
  • Entrepreneurship-focused learning for food businesses
  • Built for career growth and profitable opportunities

Meet the Founder

Chef Lyn Ewalefoh

Founder, Lyndishes Culinary School

Chef Lyn Ewalefoh is a passionate culinary professional and educator dedicated to empowering aspiring chefs and food entrepreneurs with the skills and knowledge needed to succeed in the food industry.

She is a City & Guilds certified chef who trained at the International Centre for Culinary Arts (ICCA), Dubai, one of the world’s leading culinary training institutions.

Driven by her passion for food and education, Chef Lynda founded Lyndishes Culinary School in Abuja to create a platform where people could gain not only culinary expertise, but also the business knowledge required to build successful food brands.

Through Lyndishes, she has trained thousands of students, many of whom have gone on to launch thriving catering services, restaurants, bakeries, and food brands across Nigeria.

Chef Lyn strongly believes that culinary education should go beyond cooking. Her teaching approach focuses on hands-on training, creativity, discipline, and food entrepreneurship, ensuring that every student leaves with both the skills and the confidence to build a successful career in the food industry.
